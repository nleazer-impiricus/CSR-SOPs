SOP — Detailed Steps

Search the Zendesk Knowledge Base

Open Zendesk and search for the drug name or topic. Try both the brand name and any generic name you know. Look through all matching articles for an existing documented process.

🔍
Search tips: Try different variations — e.g. "Mounjaro" and also "tirzepatide". If the first search doesn't return results, try shorter or different keywords before moving on.
🔀Did you find an existing KB article for this drug or request type?
Yes → Follow the documented process and reply to the HCP in their Zendesk ticket. You're done!
No → Continue to Step 2.

Search #concierge-call-center in Slack

Use Slack's search to look for the drug name or topic within the #concierge-call-center channel. Someone on the team may have already researched this and gotten Coordinator approval. Look for posts where a Coordinator has responded with a green light or explicit approval.

⚠️
Only use answers that have been approved by a Coordinator. If you find a post from another CSR but there's no Coordinator approval on it, do not use it — treat it as if nothing was found and continue to Step 3.
🔀Did you find a Coordinator-approved answer in Slack?
Yes → Follow that process and reply to the HCP in Zendesk.
📌
KB Gap Flag: If you found an approved process in Slack but it's not in the Zendesk KB, let a Coordinator know so they can add it. Post in #concierge-call-center: "Used Slack-approved process for [Drug Name] — not currently in KB. Flagging for KB addition."
No → Continue to Step 3.

Escalate to a Coordinator

If you couldn't find the answer in either the KB or Slack, this request needs a Coordinator. Post to #concierge-call-center using the escalation template in the Slack Posting Guide tab.

🚫
Do not search online, do not guess, and do not reply to the HCP with unverified information. In this role, anything outside the KB and Slack is outside your scope — flag it and let the Coordinator handle the research.
✅
In your Slack post, include: the drug name, the Zendesk ticket number, and a note that you searched both the KB and Slack without finding a result. The more context you give, the faster the Coordinator can help.

Once you've posted, keep an eye on the channel and follow whatever guidance the Coordinator provides. Do not reply to the HCP until you've been given a clear answer to send.

Slack Posting Guide

There are two situations where you'll need to post to #concierge-call-center. Use the right template for each.

1. Escalating a request you couldn't find an answer for

Copy and fill in this template: 🙋 ESCALATION — [Drug Name] Ticket: #[Zendesk Ticket Number] I searched the KB and #concierge-call-center but couldn't find an existing process for this request. What the HCP is asking: [Brief description of what they need — e.g. "HCP is asking how to request samples for Mounjaro"] Handing this over for Coordinator to advise. I haven't replied to the HCP yet.

2. Flagging a KB gap (found the answer in Slack but it's not in the KB)

Copy and fill in this template: 📋 KB GAP FLAG — [Drug Name] Ticket: #[Zendesk Ticket Number] I found a Coordinator-approved process for [Drug Name] in Slack (posted by [Name] on [Date]) but it's not currently in the Zendesk KB. I've used the Slack process to reply to the HCP. Flagging for KB addition so future agents can find it faster.
📌
Key rule: Never reply to an HCP with information that isn't from the KB or a Coordinator-approved Slack post. When in doubt, escalate — there's no downside to flagging, but there is a real risk to sending incorrect information.
